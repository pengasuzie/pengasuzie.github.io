How It Works

A guided interview, not a form fill

The skill walks you through nine areas of a business opportunity — asking the right questions, capturing your answers, and building a structured brief that other evaluation skills can reference.

  • 9-step interactive interview (not a form fill)
  • Reads existing opportunity context if available
  • Captures elevator pitch, problem, market, model
  • Documents key people and their commitment
  • Records confidence levels and gut check
  • Saves structured brief.md for other skills to use
> /opportunity-brief Reading opportunity context... Found: ideas/freight-automation/ Give me the 30-second version. What's the opportunity? What would the business do? > SaaS platform that automates freight forwarding document processing using AI What problem does this solve? And for whom? Who's the customer and how badly do they need this solved?
What Gets Captured

Nine areas, one structured brief

Each interview covers nine areas that together give a complete picture of the opportunity. The output is a structured markdown brief that other skills — market research, competitor analysis, viability scoring — can read and build on.

  • Elevator Pitch
  • Problem & Market
  • Business Model
  • Your Angle
  • Key People
  • Confidence & Risks
  • Resources
  • Gut Check
  • Next Steps
## Freight Automation - Brief ## Elevator Pitch SaaS platform automating freight forwarding documents with AI. ## Problem & Market Problem: Manual document processing Customer: Freight forwarders Pain level: Hair-on-fire ## Business Model Revenue: $X/month SaaS subscription Pricing: Per-document processing ## Our Angle Unfair advantage: Domain expertise Timing: AI capabilities now viable ## Gut Check Excitement (1-10): 8 Would go all-in if: First paying customer
